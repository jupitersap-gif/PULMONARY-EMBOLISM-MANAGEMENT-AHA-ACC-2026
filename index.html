👨‍⚕️ Terms of Use — Clinicians
1. Nature of this tool. PE Manager is a clinical decision support tool based on the 2026 AHA/ACC Acute PE Guidelines. It is intended to assist qualified healthcare professionals. It is not a substitute for clinical training, experience, or judgement.

2. Clinical responsibility. All clinical decisions remain the sole responsibility of the treating clinician. Every recommendation must be reviewed, verified, and adapted to the individual patient.

3. Not a regulated medical device. This tool has not been approved by the MHRA, FDA, or any regulatory body. It is provided for educational and decision support purposes only.

4. Accuracy. While every effort has been made to ensure accuracy at the time of publication (May 2026), guidelines evolve and local protocols may differ. Always verify doses against your institution's current formulary.

5. No patient data stored. This application does not collect, store, or transmit any patient identifiable information. All calculations are performed locally on your device.

6. Special populations. Exercise heightened caution for pregnant patients, paediatric patients (under 18 — this tool is not validated for paediatric use), severe renal impairment, and complex comorbidities.

7. Drug doses. All doses are guidance only. Always verify independently before prescribing, particularly in renal impairment, obesity, pregnancy, and extreme body weights.

8. Limitation of liability. The developers accept no liability for clinical decisions made using this tool. Use constitutes acceptance of full professional responsibility for all clinical decisions.
🧑‍🤝‍🧑 Information for Patients
This app is for healthcare professionals only. If you are a patient or member of the public, please be aware:

Not for self-diagnosis. Do not use this tool to assess or manage your own health or medication.

Medical emergency. If you or someone is experiencing sudden breathlessness, chest pain, collapse, or rapid heartbeat — call 999 (UK) / 112 (EU) / 911 (US) immediately.

Your data. This application does not collect any personal or identifiable information about patients. No data entered is stored or transmitted.

Questions about your care. Please speak directly with your treating doctor or nurse.

Major Haemorrhage Protocol
1
Stop anticoagulant immediately
Document last dose, time, and drug name. Do not give next dose.
2
Activate major haemorrhage protocol
Call haematology on-call. Alert transfusion lab. IV access ×2 large bore.
3
Bloods urgently
FBC, coagulation screen (PT, aPTT, fibrinogen), anti-Xa level (LMWH/DOAC), group & save.
4
Identify bleeding source
GI: endoscopy. Surgical: theatre. ICH: CT head + neurosurgery. Retroperitoneal: CT abdomen.
5
Reversal agent — see Reversal Agents tab
DOACs (anti-Xa): Andexanet alfa. Dabigatran: Idarucizumab. UFH/LMWH: Protamine. Warfarin: 4F-PCC + Vit K.
6
Blood products as needed
pRBC if Hb falling. FFP if coagulopathic (INR >1.5). Platelets if <50. Fibrinogen if <1.5 g/L (give cryoprecipitate or fibrinogen concentrate).
⚖️Critical tradeoff: Reversing anticoagulation increases PE recurrence risk. Discuss with haematology whether full reversal is truly needed vs. temporary hold for minor bleeding.
